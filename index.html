The Approach

Customer-first AI. Not AI-first.

Technology should remove friction, not relationships. I start with the customer journey, the operating model, and the decisions teams need to make—then apply AI and automation where they improve speed, accuracy, visibility, and adoption.

My work usually begins where handoffs break, knowledge is scattered, reporting is reactive, and teams are spending time on work that should already be handled by the system.

AI does not create great customer experiences. People do.

What gets optimized for cost

  • Chatbots that cannot actually help
  • Phone trees that go nowhere
  • Hallucinated or incomplete answers
  • No human available when it matters
  • Automation that sounds automated

What I build for experience

  • AI that removes busywork, not people
  • Humans present at high-value moments
  • Answers grounded in real knowledge
  • Clear ownership and escalation paths
  • Systems that stay invisible to customers
